# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Mostly students preparing for Cambridge O Level / A Level exams, browsing on phones, looking to find their subject fast, see proof the institute gets results, and eventually sign up / buy notes. Parents are a secondary audience who may glance over a student's shoulder to check credibility before a purchase or sign-up decision, but they are not the primary visitor.

A second surface (a logged-in student dashboard for purchased notes and video content) is planned but not yet built; treat the current site as the brand/marketing surface and revisit register when that dashboard work starts.

## Product Purpose

Forte Institute is a Cambridge O Level / A Level tuition provider. The site exists to: help a student quickly find their exam level and subject, convince them (and any parent watching) the institute gets real results, and convert that interest into a free trial sign-up or a notes purchase. Success looks like a student landing on the homepage, choosing their level within seconds, and reaching a subject or sign-up action without friction.

## Brand Personality

Confident, modern, sharp. Not soft, not decorative, not cutesy. The site should read like a institute that backs its results with data, not one trying to look "friendly" or "fun" to compensate for thin credibility.

## Anti-references

- Generic EdTech SaaS: cream/pastel backgrounds, bright gradients, stock-photo smiling-students imagery, identical icon+heading+text card grids, hero-metric-with-gradient-accent clichés.
- Traditional/stuffy institution: heavy serif typography, formal school-prospectus layouts, dated visual language.
- No named reference site; direction is inferred from "confident, modern, sharp" rather than copying a specific product.

## Design Principles

- Lead with the level choice (O Level / A Level), not with institutional throat-clearing — a student should reach their subject in one click from the homepage.
- Every credibility signal (stats, results, teacher quals) must read as proof, not decoration — no inflating visual weight beyond what the data itself earns.
- Sharp and confident over soft and friendly: prefer assertive type and deliberate restraint to pastel softness or playful illustration.
- Design for phones first; most traffic is students browsing on mobile, not parents at a desktop.
- Treat the current build as brand/marketing only; do not bleed dashboard/product patterns (data tables, sidebars, app-shell chrome) into these pages.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA: body text ≥4.5:1 contrast, large/bold text ≥3:1, full keyboard navigation, and a `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative for every animation (including the 3D hero scene). No other known specific user needs at this time.
